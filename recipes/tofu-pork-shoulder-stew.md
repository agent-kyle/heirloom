## Ingredients

- 1 lb pork shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1 block firm tofu, cut into cubes
- 1 cup aged kimchi (the funkier the better)
- 4 cups chicken or pork stock
- 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
- 1 tbsp gochujang
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 bunch scallions, sliced
- 1 tsp sugar

## Instructions

1. Season pork with salt. Sear in a Dutch oven in neutral oil until browned on all sides (~5 min). Remove.
2. In the same pot, cook kimchi for 2-3 min until it starts to caramelize. Add garlic, gochugaru, gochujang — stir 30 seconds.
3. Add pork back, pour in stock, soy sauce, sugar. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer. Cover and cook 45-60 min until pork is fork tender.
4. Add tofu cubes, simmer uncovered 10 more minutes.
5. Finish with sesame oil and scallions.

Serve over rice.

## Notes

- Scales up easily — multiply all ingredients proportionally (e.g. 3.5 lb pork serves ~14).
- **Broth amount:** 4 cups yields a soupier stew. For a thicker consistency, cut to 2.5–3 cups and/or simmer with the lid off at the end to reduce.
- **Freezer friendly:** If freezing, leave the tofu out of the portions you're freezing (it gets spongy). Add fresh tofu when you reheat.
