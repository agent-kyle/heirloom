## Ingredients

- 1 block (14 oz) firm tofu
- 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed into bite-sized pieces
- 2 cans (14 oz each) coconut milk
- 4 tbsp red curry paste
- 4 tbsp vegetable or coconut oil
- 4 cups fresh spinach or baby spinach (8 oz)
- 4 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp lime juice (about 1 lime)
- 2 tsp brown sugar
- Cooked jasmine or white rice for serving

**Optional:**
- Mushrooms (sliced) — add with curry paste in step 3
- Broccoli (florets) — add with sweet potato in step 4

**Optional garnishes:** fresh cilantro, crushed peanuts, sliced green onions

## Instructions

1. **Press the tofu.** Wrap block in paper towels or a clean kitchen towel and place something heavy on top (cast iron pan, canned goods). Let sit 15-20 minutes to drain excess water.
2. **Cook the tofu.** Cut pressed tofu into cubes. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add half the tofu and cook, turning occasionally, until golden on most sides — about 8-10 minutes. Remove and set aside. Repeat with remaining oil and tofu.
3. **Build the curry.** In the same pan, add curry paste (and mushrooms if using). Stir and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Pour in coconut milk and stir until smooth.
4. **Cook the sweet potato.** Add cubed sweet potato (and broccoli if using) to the curry. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook 12-15 minutes until sweet potato is fork-tender.
5. **Finish the dish.** Stir in soy sauce, lime juice, and brown sugar. Add tofu back to the pan along with spinach. Stir gently until spinach wilts, about 1-2 minutes.
6. **Serve.** Spoon over rice and top with cilantro, crushed peanuts, or green onions if desired.

## Notes

- Pressing the tofu is key — it helps it crisp up when cooking.
- Easily customizable with whatever vegetables you have on hand.
