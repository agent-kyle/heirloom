## Ingredients

- 2 lbs (900g) bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 1/4 cup Asian fish sauce (Red Boat recommended)
- 1/4 cup palm sugar or dark brown sugar
- 2 tbsp grapeseed or neutral oil
- 2 packed tbsp fresh cilantro, finely chopped
- 1 1/2 tbsp fresh lime juice (1 lime)
- 1 tsp finely grated lime zest
- 1 tbsp minced fresh ginger
- 2 tsp hot chile paste (sambal oelek)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- Sliced limes and cilantro, for garnish

## Instructions

1. Place thighs in a large zipper-lock bag. Whisk together soy sauce, fish sauce, sugar, oil, cilantro, lime juice and zest, ginger, chile paste, and garlic. Pour marinade into bag, seal, and toss to coat. Marinate 30 min to 4 hours (no longer — acid will make meat mushy).
2. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil, place a wire rack on top.
3. Remove chicken from bag, let marinade drip off (don't rinse). Place skin-side up on rack with space between thighs. Discard marinade.
4. Bake until thighs register 155–160°F in the thickest part (not against bone), about 35 minutes.
5. Optional: broil on high for ~1 minute to crisp and brown the skin. Watch carefully — the sugar in the marinade can burn.
6. Rest 5 minutes. Garnish with lime slices and cilantro.

## Notes

- Serve with steamed rice and quick-pickled cucumbers and red onions.
- Palm sugar comes in rock-hard bricks — grate with a box grater or soften in hot water. Dark brown sugar is a fine substitute.
- Fish sauce is the key ingredient here — it adds deep umami without tasting fishy.
