## Ingredients

- 1 lb dried black beans, rinsed (no soaking needed)
- 8 oz Mexican chorizo, removed from casing
- 1 whole onion, split in half
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 1 orange, split in half
- 2 bay leaves
- 1.5 quarts chicken stock
- 2 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp vegetable or olive oil

## Instructions

1. Heat oil in pressure cooker until shimmering. Add chorizo and cook, stirring, until it releases its fat and starts to crisp, about 2 minutes.
2. Add beans, onion, garlic cloves, orange, bay leaves, chicken stock, and 2 teaspoons salt.
3. Lock lid and bring to high pressure. Cook at high pressure for 40 minutes.
4. Release pressure naturally (about 10-15 minutes), then open lid.
5. Remove and discard onion, orange halves, and bay leaves.
6. Season to taste with additional salt. Beans should be tender and creamy.

## Notes

- No soaking required — the pressure cooker does all the work.
- Great for meal prep — makes a big batch. Pairs perfectly with rice, tacos, or burrito bowls.
- Freezes well for up to 3 months.
- For a vegetarian version, skip the chorizo and use vegetable stock.
- The orange adds a subtle sweetness and helps tenderize the beans.
