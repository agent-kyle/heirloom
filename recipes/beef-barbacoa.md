## Ingredients

- 6 lb beef chuck roast
- 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce (reduced for toddler)
- 2 cups beef broth
- 6 tbsp lime juice
- 4 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 10 garlic cloves
- 2 white onions, roughly chopped
- 4 tsp ground cumin
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp ground cloves
- 4 bay leaves
- 2 tsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 4 tbsp olive oil

## Instructions

1. Blend chipotle peppers, broth, lime juice, vinegar, garlic, onion, cumin, oregano, smoked paprika, and ground cloves until smooth.
2. Cut chuck roast into 4 large chunks. Season with salt and pepper. Sear in olive oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat, 3-4 minutes per side. Work in batches.
3. Pour blended sauce over seared beef. Add bay leaves. Liquid should nearly cover the meat. Bring to a simmer, cover, and transfer to a 300°F oven.
4. After 3 hours, remove bay leaves and shred beef with two forks in the pot. Stir to combine with braising juices.
5. Optional: simmer uncovered on stovetop 10-15 minutes to reduce and thicken sauce.
6. Serve as tacos, burrito bowls, or quesadillas.

## Notes

- Toddler-friendly: reduced chipotles from 6 to 2. Can pull toddler portion before reducing sauce.
- Slow cooker option: LOW 8-9 hours or HIGH 5-6 hours.
- Makes great leftovers — freezes up to 3 months.
- Burrito bowl bar: set out rice, beans, corn, cheese, avocado, cilantro, and let everyone build their own.
